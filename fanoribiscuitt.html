Fenori recipe

Makes: 12 fenoris

Preparation Time: 30 minutes

Cooking Time: 2-3 minutes per fenori

Ingredients:

  1. All purpose flour (Maida): 3/4 th cup
  2. Oil : 3 tbsp
  3. Rice Flour : 4-5 tsp
  4. Water : for kneading
  5. Oil : for frying
  6. Powdered Sugar : 1/4 th cup (you can increase or decrease this)
  7. Salt : as per taste (just around 2-3 pinches)

Method of Preparation:

  1. Add the maida, water, warm oil and salt and knead into a dough. Knead well as the dough needs to be soft.
  2. Divide the dough into 3 spherical balls. Roll out each maida ball into a thin circular shape. Keep each one on a separate plate. Keeping the rolled discs one over other may result in them sticking to each other.
  3. Now take one of the rolled circle on a flat surface. Take some ghee(around 1 tsp) and apply uniformly on the circular disc.
  4. Now sprinkle rice flour(around 3/4th tsp) uniformly on the ghee smeared disc. You just need to lightly sprinkle the rice flour and not really have a thick layer of it.
  5. Take the second disc and place it on the first disc. Now apply ghee and sprinkle rice flour on this layer and gently place the third circular disc on it.
  6. Once again lightly apply ghee on the top layer. Now gently roll up this 3 layered disc (refer pic).
  7. With a knife cut the roll into 10-12 parts. (refer pic 9 and 10)
  8. Roll out each part into a 6 cm diameter disc. You may need to smear some maida for rolling the discs.
  9. Heat oil in a wok and deep fry each of the discs till they are slightly golden in color.
  10. Remove from oil and place on an absorbent paper. Once it cools slightly sprinkle powdered sugar on each and your crispy fenori is ready. Do not sprinkle sugar immediately after getting the fenori out of oil. As this can result in the fenori turning soft.
