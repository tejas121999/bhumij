Mustard

Mustard powders and pastes are used to thicken the gravy. The seeds along with oil and other herbs and spices are also used to add tadka to dal or vegetables. Mustard is generally stored as seeds instead of paste as they last longer that way.

Cumin Seed, Certified Organic

Whole seeds can be toasted or bloomed in ghee to release the flavor. Use cumin with meat or vegetable dishes, mushrooms, cream sauce, salad dressing, and summer beverages. Cumin pairs well with coriander, other warming spices, chilies, and fresh herbs.

Oregano

Oregano comes from the mint family and has quite a robust flavour. Unlike other herbs and spices, oregano has a pre conceived notion of being a pizza herb! It has many other uses which is now slowly coming to the Indian kitchen.

Coriander Seed, Certified Organic

Coriander has a citrusy, nutty flavor and aroma, which is heightened by dry-roasting. Use in breads, stews, soups, pickled vegetables, and condiments. Pairs well with cumin.
